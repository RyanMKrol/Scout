# T049 — Uncap the readout and retune the dial scale to real speeds

## 2026-07-20

**Did:**
- `Sources/Domain/ScoutMeter.swift`: removed `downloadCapMbps`/`uploadCapMbps` and the "10+"/"5+"
  peg from `downloadDisplay`/`uploadDisplay` — now format the real Mbps (one decimal below 10, a
  rounded whole number at/above it). Replaced the capped log scales in
  `downloadArcFraction`/`uploadArcFraction` with open-ended log scales against new
  `downloadScaleMbps` (300) / `uploadScaleMbps` (100) constants, still clamped to `[0.04, 1.0]`
  and monotonic past those scale ceilings (the arc simply pins at 1.0 for faster readings).
- `Sources/Domain/SweepSession.swift`: removed the `min(value, ScoutMeter.*CapMbps)` clamps in
  `record` and `republishFromWindow` — `downloadMbps`/`uploadMbps` now publish the real windowed
  value.

  cap-phrasing branches; down/up are always announced as their real rounded value.
- `Sources/DesignSystem/DialCenterStack.swift`: updated a `#Preview`'s literal placeholder
  strings ("10+"/"5+" → "142"/"48") — no functional dial/tick constants in DesignSystem assumed
  the old 0–10/0–5 range, so nothing else needed retuning there.
- `SignalQuality` thresholds (Great ≥ 6 / Usable ≥ 2 / Poor < 2) were left untouched — they already
  operate on the uncapped Mbps value.
- Tests updated/added (`Tests/DomainTests.swift`, `Tests/SweepSessionTests.swift`,
  `Tests/AccessibilityTests.swift`, `Tests/AccessibilitySummaryTests.swift`): display formatting
  above the old caps now asserts the real number (e.g. 42.5 → "43", not "10+"); arc-fraction
  tests assert monotonicity and `[0.04, 1.0]` clamping across low and high (up to 300/100 Mbps)

  (40.0 / 160.0) instead of the old capped constants; added
  `testSignalQualityAgainstUncappedValues` pinning the quality-band mapping still holds above the
  old caps. One `SweepSessionTests` assertion (fresh sample after a stall) was loosened from an
  exact Mbps match to `> 0`, since without a cap the exact resumed value depends on real
  wall-clock spacing between the rolling-window's still-buffered stale sample and the fresh one —
  a timing artifact of `ThroughputWindow`, not something this task's scope covers fixing.
