# T048 — Periodic upload bursts alongside the continuous download

## Overview
The continuous download (T045) saturates the downlink, so upload can't be measured the same
continuous way. To keep an upload reading (owner decision: keep upload), measure it in short periodic
bursts on a separate cellular-bound connection, feeding the existing upload window/readout.

## Do
Add a periodic upload-burst path to the sampler (`Sources/Live/**`): on an interval (e.g. every few
seconds — a single named constant), run a short upload burst over a SEPARATE forced-`.cellular`
`NetworkConnection` (so it doesn't disturb the continuous download connection), timing the burst, and
`yield` upload `ThroughputSample`(s) for it. The upload burst may keep the transfer-time-based timing
it already had (a burst is a bounded transfer, so its own duration is the right divisor) — record it
into the upload window in whatever form T046's window accepts (`byteCount` + arrival instant per
chunk, or a single burst sample). Keep bursts small and infrequent enough that upload data stays a
minor fraction of total use (the download stream dominates). Preserve the `ThroughputSampler` seam and
update the fake so upload samples still appear in tests.

## Done when
- While a sweep runs, upload is measured via periodic bursts on a separate `.cellular` connection that
  does not interrupt the continuous download stream, and the upload readout (`uploadMbps`) updates from
  those bursts.
- A unit test (fake + injected clock) asserts upload bursts are emitted on the configured interval and
  update the upload reading, while the continuous download stream keeps flowing.
- Upload data volume stays a small fraction of the session total (bursts are bounded and periodic, not
  continuous).
- On device (manual, T025-style): the upload number updates periodically without stalling the download
  reading.
