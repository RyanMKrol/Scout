## Overview
Add a tap gesture on the central dial in the live measuring view that toggles pause/resume via SweepSession, with a clear paused visual state and a stable accessibility identifier.

## Do
In the live measuring content (MeasuringView.measuringContent / SweepDialView live mode), add a tap target over the central dial that calls the pause/resume toggle added in unit A. When session.isPaused is true, show a clear paused visual indication on the dial and change the status row from 'SWEEPING' to a paused affordance — but do NOT reuse the empty-state 'paused.status' identifier (that is the no-consent/no-cellular state, which must keep working unchanged). Give the paused status/indicator a NEW stable accessibilityIdentifier (e.g. `dial.paused`) and give the tappable dial area a stable identifier (e.g. `dial.pauseToggle`). The gesture must only be active in the live sweep state, not in pausedContent(). Keep VoiceOver sensible: expose the pause/resume action and reflect the paused state in the accessibility value.

## Done when
Tapping the central dial while sweeping freezes the readout and reveals a paused indicator identified by a NEW accessibilityIdentifier (distinct from the existing empty-state `paused.status`); tapping again resumes live updates; the toggle is unavailable in the no-consent/no-cellular empty states (those screens are visually unchanged and their identifiers still resolve); and a new XCUITest in Tests/ launches the app in the measuring state, queries the dial toggle by accessibilityIdentifier, taps it, asserts the paused indicator appears (bounded wait, by identifier not display copy), taps again, and asserts it disappears. Pure-ui, auto-covered by the loop's visual hook.
