# T049 — Uncap the readout and retune the dial scale to real speeds

## Overview
Now that the continuous download (T045–T047) measures true sustained throughput, show the REAL Mbps
instead of the "10+" / "5+" peg. Today `Sources/Domain/ScoutMeter.swift` caps download at 10 and upload
at 5 (`downloadCapMbps`/`uploadCapMbps`), `downloadDisplay`/`uploadDisplay` return "10+"/"5+", the arc
fractions are log scales fitted to those capped ranges, and `SweepSession.record` clamps
`min(value, ScoutMeter.downloadCapMbps)`. This removes the cap end-to-end and retunes the dial so it
reads sensibly across real cellular speeds, keeping the Great/Usable/Poor quality bands.

## Do
Remove the display cap: in `Sources/Domain/ScoutMeter.swift` make `downloadDisplay`/`uploadDisplay`
show the actual measured Mbps (no "10+"/"5+" peg — format sensibly, e.g. one decimal below 10 and a
rounded integer at/above 10), and retune `downloadArcFraction`/`uploadArcFraction` to an open-ended log
scale spanning realistic cellular throughput (e.g. up to a few hundred Mbps) while staying monotonic and
clamped to `[0.04, 1.0]`. Remove the `min(value, cap)` clamps in `SweepSession.record` (and anywhere
else the reading is capped) so the published `downloadMbps`/`uploadMbps` are the real values. Keep the
quality bands unchanged (`SignalQuality`: Great ≥ 6 / Usable ≥ 2 / Poor < 2, download-only) — they
still map onto the uncapped number. Retune any dial tick/scale constants in `Sources/DesignSystem/**`
that assumed the 0–10 / 0–5 range.

## Done when
- The readout shows the real measured Mbps (no "10+"/"5+" peg); values above 10 down / 5 up display the
  actual number, and the reading is no longer clamped in `SweepSession`.
- The dial arc scale is retuned to real speeds: `downloadArcFraction`/`uploadArcFraction` are monotonic,
  clamped to `[0.04, 1.0]`, and give a sensible sweep across ~0 to a few hundred Mbps.
- The Great/Usable/Poor bands still classify correctly against the uncapped value.
- `Tests/DomainTests.swift` (or `ScoutMeter`'s tests) assert: a >10 Mbps value formats as its real
  number (not "10+"), arc fractions stay monotonic + in range across low and high speeds, and the
  quality band mapping is unchanged.
- On device (manual, T025-style): a fast connection shows its true speed (e.g. ~40 Mbps), not "10+".
